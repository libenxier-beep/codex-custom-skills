# Principles

## Preserve Claim Boundaries

- claim: A reusable claim should stay tied to the source conditions that made it credible.
- mechanism: Claims lose reliability when the agent strips away audience, constraints, method, and counterevidence.
- use_when:
  - Distilling multiple sources that mostly agree.
  - Turning a case into reusable operating knowledge.
- do_not_use_when:
  - The source is a weak anecdote with no transferable mechanism.

## Downgrade Weak Viral Sources

- claim: A vivid viral source can seed an open question, but should not become a high-confidence principle by itself.
- mechanism: Viral material is often optimized for memorability, not sampling quality, counterexamples, or transfer boundaries.
- use_when:
  - A source is rhetorically strong but evidence-light.
- do_not_use_when:
  - The viral source is only being used as a quote or style sample.
